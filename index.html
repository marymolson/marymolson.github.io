I'm a Marshall Scholar and MSc Economics student at the London School of Economics (LSE) with interests in labor and applied microeconomics. I'm particularly interested in leveraging linked microdata to examine how technological shocks, policy changes, and economic change shape individual and regional worker outcomes.

I hold a BA from Columbia University in Economics and an MSc in Economic History from LSE. My master's thesis received the LSE prize for best MSc Economic History dissertation.
